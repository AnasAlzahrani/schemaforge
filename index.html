2.Study design types

Select the structure that best matches your question.

RCT

Randomized Controlled Trial

Gold standard for causal inference when ethical equipoise and blinding are feasible.

Cohort

Prospective or retrospective

Follow exposed vs unexposed over time to estimate incidence and relative risk.

Case-Control

Retrospective comparison

Efficient for rare outcomes. Careful control selection is critical.

Cross-Sectional

Point-in-time snapshot

Useful for prevalence and hypothesis generation; temporality is limited.

3.Methods appendix

Common causal inference methods explained in plain terms.

  • PSM: Match treated and untreated on propensity scores to reduce confounding.
  • IPW: Reweight observations to emulate a randomized experiment.
  • IV: Use an instrument correlated with treatment but not with the outcome directly.
  • RDD: Exploit sharp assignment cutoffs for quasi-experimental estimation.
